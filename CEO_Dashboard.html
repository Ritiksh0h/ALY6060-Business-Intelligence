Dashboard findings — linked to business partner argument
Each finding maps to a peer-reviewed source from the written report

Finding 1 — internal hires outperform on growth: Internal hire CEOs averaged +9.6% revenue growth versus −4.7% for external hires. Longer tenure (10.1 yrs vs 4.7 yrs) allows embedded strategic HR structures to mature — consistent with Wach et al. (2022) on the mediating role of internal efficiency.

Finding 2 — confounding variable warning: All 3 external hires are women in healthcare and finance, industries facing structural headwinds in 2024 (Medicare Advantage reform, Medicaid pressure). Industry effects cannot be fully separated from hire-type effects at n=10.

Finding 3 — compensation does not predict performance: Nadella ($79.1M, +16%) and Lynch ($12M, −5%) show that pay level does not drive growth. Structural competency and organizational support matter more — supports Tactic 2 from the report (McCracken et al., 2017).

Finding 4 — pay disparity amplifies capture risk: Walmart's 930:1 ratio and GM's 310:1 illustrate environments where HR business partners face intense political pressure. Structural independence via dual reporting lines (Tactic 1) becomes critical — consistent with Gerpott (2015).
